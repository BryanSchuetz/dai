Eduardo González-Pier is an economist with more than 20 years of experience in the health and social security sectors. He contributed to the development of health policy in Mexico, including major reforms, from varied high-level positions at the Ministry of Health and the Mexican Institute of Social Security.
•	Work experience has focused on providing sound evidence and analysis to inform policymaking and promote the development of mechanisms and tools to improve health system performance.

PhD in Economics, The University of Chicago, Chicago IL, USA 
Dissertation: “Dynamic Gains from Trade Liberalization: The case of Mexico, concentrated on public finance, economic growth and international trade”. 
MA in Economics, The University of Chicago, Chicago IL, USA 
Bachelor of Science with majors in Engineering, Economics and Mathematics, Washington and Lee University, VA, USA. Scholarship by the University. Honors - Magna Cum Laude.
